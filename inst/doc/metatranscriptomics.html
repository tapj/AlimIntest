Introduction

Four subjects were subjected to 10g of fiber diet per day first then 40g fiber per diet after a washout period of 15 days. In addition to 16S rRNA gene sequencing, metatranscriptomics was also done from stool samples. Here we aimed to compare gut microbiota composition, taxonomic and functional activies.

Conclusion

  • Microbial composition and activities are linked. Main difference might be due to the metatranscriptomics reads annotation biaised by reference database.

  • A coinertia analysis combined with a partial tridiac analysis allowed us to decipher the link between taxonomic and functional activities across diet change. Lachnospiraceae, Eubacteriaceae and other Firmicutes were linked to glycan metabolism and energy metabolism. Bacteroides and Proteobacteria were linked translation and metabolism of cofactors and vitamins. This link is stronger after 10g of fiber compared to 40g.
